Paul Scotti

Cognitive Neuroscience Undergraduate

I've become captivated by the mechanisms underlying visual attention and neuroscience in general for the past several years.
I have been working on research related to real-world object-based attention and predictive visual search.
Besides research, I enjoy meditation, producing music, and playing board games!

How I Got Here

I have always had a fascination with the brain, as evidenced by my years of becoming a guinea pig for my own amusement.
Some endeavors include lucid dreaming, tDCS self-stimulation, nootropics, mnemonics/memory palaces, and keeping an excel doc of all media consumption.

I use Cerego, a spaced repetition website, to maintain knowledge of cog neuro topics and important research articles. You can check out my spaced repetition database here, and my prototype wikipedia page of neuro notes here. (I've since replaced it with OneNote.)

My experience in research comes from independent projects working with Dr. Sarah Shomstein and Dr. Stephen Mitroff.
I am proficient in MATLAB for experimental design as well as data analysis.
